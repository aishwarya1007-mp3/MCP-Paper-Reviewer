ACADEMIC PAPER REVIEW CHECKLIST
(Target venue: Top-tier CS / ML / Crypto conference such as ICLR, NeurIPS, CCS)

--------------------------------------------------
1. PROBLEM FORMULATION & MOTIVATION
--------------------------------------------------
- Is the problem clearly defined and well-scoped?
- Is the motivation compelling and relevant to the target research community?
- Is it clear why this problem matters now?
- Are the limitations of existing approaches explicitly stated?

--------------------------------------------------
2. NOVELTY & ORIGINALITY
--------------------------------------------------
- Does the paper introduce a genuinely new idea, method, or theoretical insight?
- Is the contribution incremental or substantially different from prior work?
- Are the differences from closely related work clearly articulated?
- Does the paper risk being a re-packaging or minor variation of known techniques?

--------------------------------------------------
3. RELATED WORK & CITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------
- Is the related work comprehensive and up to date?
- Are seminal papers in the area cited?
- Are recent (last 3–5 years) top-conference works included?
- Are comparisons to the most relevant baselines present?
- Are there claims that lack appropriate citations?

--------------------------------------------------
4. TECHNICAL SOUNDNESS
--------------------------------------------------
- Are the assumptions clearly stated and justified?
- Are the definitions precise and unambiguous?
- Are proofs (if any) correct, complete, and well-structured?
- Are there missing steps, hand-wavy arguments, or unjustified leaps?
- Does the methodology logically follow from the assumptions?

--------------------------------------------------
5. METHODOLOGY / THEORY / EXPERIMENTS
--------------------------------------------------
(Apply what is relevant)

- Is the proposed method clearly described and reproducible?
- Are experimental setups fair and appropriate?
- Are baselines well-chosen and correctly implemented?
- Are ablation studies or theoretical justifications sufficient?
- Are negative results or failure cases discussed?

--------------------------------------------------
6. CLARITY & PRESENTATION
--------------------------------------------------
- Is the paper well-written and easy to follow?
- Are figures, tables, and diagrams clear and informative?
- Is notation consistent throughout the paper?
- Are key ideas obscured by unnecessary complexity?

--------------------------------------------------
7. CLAIMS & CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------
- Do the conclusions follow logically from the results?
- Are there over-claims or exaggerated statements?
- Are limitations discussed honestly?
- Does the paper clearly state its scope and boundaries?

--------------------------------------------------
8. IMPACT & SIGNIFICANCE
--------------------------------------------------
- If accepted, would this paper influence future research?
- Does it open new directions or significantly improve existing ones?
- Is the contribution mainly theoretical, practical, or both?
- Who is the target audience?

--------------------------------------------------
9. REPRODUCIBILITY & ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS
--------------------------------------------------
- Are sufficient details provided to reproduce the results?
- Are datasets, code, or parameters described adequately?
- Are there any ethical concerns or misuse risks?
- Are claims responsibly framed?

--------------------------------------------------
10. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------
- Strengths (list 2–3 major strengths)
- Weaknesses (list 2–3 major weaknesses)
- Overall novelty score (0–10)
- Confidence in the review (low / medium / high)
- Final recommendation:
  - Accept
  - Weak Accept
  - Weak Reject
  - Reject
